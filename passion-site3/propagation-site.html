Some of these propogations have been taken from my own plants, my mom's plants, and friends' plants. I also have collected clippings from walking around downtown Richmond. Different plants are propogated in different ways, but th emajority of my clippings have been taken near a node. It's very important to clip right under the node in order to have a succesful propogation. Some clippings will take a few days to a few months to start growing roots. It just takes patience. Once the roots are about 6-10 inches long, then they can be put in soil to conitnue flourishing.
